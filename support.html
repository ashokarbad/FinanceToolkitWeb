No. The app works 100% offline and does not require a sign-up. Your data stays on your device. If you enable iCloud sync, your data syncs through your personal Apple iCloud account — we never see it.
Finance Toolkit uses Apple's iCloud Key-Value Store. As long as you're signed in to the same Apple ID on each device and have iCloud enabled in Settings, your calculations, expenses, outflows, payments, and notes sync automatically. Initial sync can take a minute or two.
Make sure biometric authentication is enabled in Settings > Face ID & Passcode on your device, and that Finance Toolkit has permission to use it. If biometrics fail repeatedly, the app will fall back to your device passcode. You can toggle the lock on or off inside Finance Toolkit's Settings.
Check that notifications are allowed for Finance Toolkit under iOS Settings > Notifications. Reminders are scheduled for 9:00 AM on the day, 1 day before, or 3 days before the due date — depending on your preference inside the app. Notifications require a physical device (they don't fire on the Simulator).
Open Settings > Data Export inside the app to export a full backup as a JSON file. You can save it to Files, iCloud Drive, or share it elsewhere. To restore, use Data Import and choose either Merge (combine with existing data) or Replace (overwrite).
Yes. The app supports 11 currencies (INR, USD, EUR, GBP, AED, SAR, CAD, AUD, SGD, JPY, DOP). You can change the currency at any time from within Settings, and the change applies instantly.
The calculators use standard financial formulas (EMI, compound interest, CAGR, etc.) which are universally applicable. Tax calculators currently follow Indian Income Tax slabs (Old vs New regime). For country-specific rules outside India, treat the results as estimates and confirm with a financial professional.
Open Expenses > Scan Receipt and either take a photo with your camera or pick an image from your library. The app uses Apple's on-device Vision OCR to extract the total amount, merchant name, and date, then auto-categorises the expense into one of 27 categories. Everything runs locally — no image leaves your device. If the OCR misreads a character (for example the ₹ symbol as a "7"), the app automatically detects and strips the stray character before saving the amount.
Yes. Go to Expenses > Import Bank Statement and select a PDF or CSV file from your bank. The app parses transactions (UPI, NEFT, IMPS, ECS, and more) and auto-categorises each one using a 300+ keyword engine across 27 categories. You can review and edit categories before adding the transactions as expenses. The app also learns from your corrections — recategorise once and it remembers next time. All parsing happens on your device.
Six widgets across Home Screen and Lock Screen: Monthly Summary (expense + outflow totals with savings indicator), Upcoming Payments (next dues with countdown), Quick Expense (month total with top category), Spending Categories (top 5 with proportion bars), Monthly Outflow (Lock Screen), and Scan Receipt (tap to open camera). Long-press your Home Screen, tap "+", search for Finance Toolkit, and pick a size.
